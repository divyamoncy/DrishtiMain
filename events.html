Line Follower

The competition is conducted as two levels. In the first level, the bot must complete a line track in minimum time. The track consists of acute angles, obtuse angles, right angles and smooth curves. The teams completing the 1 st round will qualify for the second round. The second round is a ‘Trap the Rat’ track. This round consists of a line following track with black and white stripes blocking the path, representing rats and rat traps respectively. The bot must indicate the detection of the rat and the trap by sounding a buzzer/LED. The objective of the bot is to count the number of rats and rat traps and to indicate the number of free rats (no. of rats - no. of traps) using a buzzer/LED. Points will be awarded in the second round based on the following formula: Points = (600-T) + (50*I) – 10*P
T - Total time taken to complete the track, in seconds.
I - Number of correct detection of rat and trap.
P - Number of times the bot goes off track.

Additional 10 points will be awarded if the indication of final answer is correct. If no team is able to finish the second round, winners will be decided based on the number of checkpoints reached. Teams unable to complete the first round will be eliminated. Winners will be declared based on points achieved.

Arena Details


Round 1-
1. The track will be 3 cm wide.
2. The track consists of black line on white background with checkpoints.
3. There will be acute angles, obtuse angles, right angles and smooth curves.

Round 2:-
1. The track will be 3 cm wide.
2. The track consists of black line on white background.
3. The end of the track will be indicated by colour inversion, at end of track the track will be white with a black background
4. There will be acute angles, obtuse angles, right angles and smooth curves.
5. Rats and traps are as shown in the figure.
6. In case the bot goes off track, it can be reset next to the last rat or trap.

RULES AND REGULATIONS


1. Bots made using ready-made kits will be disqualified
2. The bot must fit into a cube of 20 x 20 x 20 cm.
3. Teams of four.
4. Inter college teams are allowed.

RoboSoccer

All the bots must compete in a knockout soccer tournament. The ball will be placed on the centre of the field, with the competing bots on either side protecting their goal post and trying to score goals. The teams will be divided into separate pools, details of which will announced before the tournament. 5 minutes of warm-up time will be provided to each team for pre-game setup and testing. Each match is of 6 minutes duration with an extra time of 3 minutes in case of a tie. Winners from each pool will be promoted to higher levels. At the beginning of a game, the ball is placed at the centre of the field, with the bots placed in front of their respective goal posts.

RULES AND REGULATIONS


Gameplay Rules
1.Each team is allowed to have a maximum of two bots. The bots must fit into a cube of (30x30x30) cm 3 at all times.
2.It may not expand at any time during the match beyond this size even for hitting the ball. Violating this clause will lead to immediate disqualification.
3. One wireless/wired bot can move all over the ground.
4. The wired bot can have on board as well as off board power supply but wireless bot should have an onboard power supply. Teams will be provided 220 volts 50 Hz AC supply.
5. The maximum potential difference between any two points on the bot should not exceed 24 volts.
6.A flipper for hitting the ball is allowed but not mandatory.
7. Grabbing the ball is not allowed. So, bots should not have any kind of grabbing mechanisms.
8.In case of a tie, 3 min extra time will be given. The team to score first during the extra time will win the match. If no goal is scored during the extra time, winner will be decided by penalty shoot outs during which 2 penalty shoots will be given to each team in turn. The better of two penalties will be considered to decide the winner.
9.During Penalty shoots, only one bot (i.e. the striker) will be placed in arena and ball will be placed at the centre of the arena. The team to score a goal in lesser time will be considered winner.
10. After one Half, the goalposts will be swapped.
11. A goal is scored when the ball crosses the goalpost line completely.
12.Coordinators posses the right to change part of the rules.

General Participation Rules
1.A team may consist of maximum of 5 members. Students from different institutes may form a team.
2.Ready-made LEGO kits are not allowed. However, ready-made gear assemblies and development boards may be used.
3.Teams will have to report at least 15 minutes before the time slot allotted to them at the beginning of the event, failing to do so may result in disqualification.
4.No last-minute repairs will be allowed in the arena. However, each team can take two time-out of 2 minutes each once during a match.
5.Bots would be checked for their safety before the match and may be discarded if found unsafe for other team or spectators.
6.Organizers reserve the rights to change the rules at any point of time as they deem fit.

Fouls
1.Any kind of intentional damage caused to opponent team’s bot will be considered as foul. Intentional grabbing of the ball so as to make it impossible for other team to get the control of the ball will also be considered as foul.
2. A free kick will be given to other team in case of fouls during which bots of the team committing the foul will be frozen for 30 seconds and ball will be given to the other team.
3. In case of repeated fouls, yellow card and red cards will be given. If yellow card is given the score will be reduced whereas the in case of red card bot will not be allowed to play current as well as the next match.
4. The foul committed inside the D-area will lead to a penalty.
5. Decision of the referee will be final and binding.

Disqualification Rules
Following cases will attract immediate disqualification:
1. If a team fails to adhere to size specifications.
2. If a team damages the arena in any way.
3. If a team commits repeated fouls.
4. If a team fails to report in time.
5.If a team tries to intervene the game play without permission of the referee.

DigiQuest 2.0

Participating teams are tested in their knowledge of digital logic and digital circuit design. They are given a progression of undertakings in the form of a narrative which they need to finish in least time to win the opposition.
What the students gain from participating in this event?
This is a competition to hone and test the technical knowledge of the participants in digital electronics. It servers as an opportunity for students to compete and showcase their talent in digital electronics . The competition allows the participants to integrate real life situation with digital circuits.

The event is conducted in three rounds.

LEVEL 1
This level is an elimination round in which an MCQ test is conducted on basics of digital electronics and digital logic. We expect a maximum participation of 15 teams .Only 5 teams are selected for the next round.
The test contains 40 questions and is conducted for a duration of 30 minutes. Marks will be deducted for wrong answers.
Judging criteria : Marks scored in the test

For levels 2 and 3, a question paper containing a scenario in the form of a story is provided.The aim of the levels is to help the protagonist(s) of the narration achieve a goal.

LEVEL 2
In this level the input and output of a digital circuit are provided to the participants. Also the exact components of the circuit are given to each team.Participants have to analyse the output and design the circuit within an hour . The first three teams to complete this round move on to the third level.

Judging criteria : The time taken to complete the circuit.

LEVEL 3
In this level lasting for a maximum of 2 hours, a question and an incomplete circuit is given to the teams. Each team has to complete the circuit and accomplish the specified task to succeed in this round.In the event of more than one team finishing the task,the team which completed the task in the least time is declared the winner.
Judging criteria : The time taken to complete the circuit and the logic used.

RULES AND REGULATIONS


1.This is a team event. A team can have 2-5 members
2.Students of any institution can take part.
3.Students should bring valid ID cards.
4. The decision of the coordinators will be final and binding in all respects.
5.Use of internet or any form of cheating during the event will lead to automatic disqualification.

ACCURACY TEST
The team will be asked to launch the missiles onto a specific target. There will be targets at specific ranges. The targets will be at 50, 60, 70, 80 meters(changes might be made by organizer) and points are awarded as said by organizer and a total of 120 points will be produced as a part of the two chances provided.
