LoRa

Bored with the traditional Internet of Things(IOT) workshops and lectures? Ever wondered how you would be able to create a parallel internet or how you would be able to run a node for about 5 years with a 5V / 50MAh battery? Well, here’s your one stop for everything.

For the first time in the state, RoboCET presents Introduction to LoRa Workshop in association with Drishti, the technical fest of College of Engineering Trivandrum.

LoRa is a brand new communication protocol that introduced extremely low power, low cost modules for communication over distances of 15 to 20 km. Perfect for all designs involving Internet of Things and distributed node networks, the methods and device designs of LoRa is a closely guarded secret, accessible only to very few people working within its niche industry.
The applications for LoRa are endless, with low-hanging fruit at industries in Internet of Things, Smart meters, Automotive industry, in fact, anywhere data reporting and control is used.

Topics covered:
1.Introduction to LoRa
2.WiFi, Bluetooth vs LoRa- which better?
3.Setting up LoRa gateway and connecting to the things network
4.Communication with LoRa gateway
5.Real time app of Lora

No prior knowledge is expected.
Certificates will be provided for each workshop applicant.
Kits will be provided for each group
(Development Boards would be taken back).

Register now, as a team of five, to be a part of this two day workshop, where you will gain an insight in LoRa communications, and build your own system using a LoRa supported, microcontroller system.
Seats will be provided on first-come, first-serve basis.

Embedded Systems using Atmega

The greatest achievement of technology in the present era is the miniaturization of products.Ever wondered what makes complex circuits small and what lies beneath them all? The answer to this is Embedded Systems. From gaming consoles to spacecrafts, it would be difficult to find an area where embedded systems are not used.

As part of Drishti by College of Engineering Trivandrum, IEEE SB CET presents before you, a workshop on Embedded Systems using Atmega. Here is your chance to dive into the world of embedded systems with one of the most popular microcontrollers, the Atmega32. Atmega32 belongs to Atmel's AVR series of microcontrollers.The workshop covers all relevant topics including Atmega32 architecture, introduction to Atmel Studio, embedded C programming for Atmega32 and so on .The afternoon session will see you making some practical stuff out of the things you have studied , multimeters, thermometers etc..to name a few.
